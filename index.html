Medulloblastoma Outcome Predictor

Medulloblastoma, a malignant brain tumor affecting mainly pediatric populations, poses significant challenges in terms of accurate clinical outcome prediction. In my studies I undertook the challenge of a re-analysis of gene expression data from the study by Pomeroy et al, "Prediction of central nervous system embryonal tumour outcome based on gene expression", Nature 2002, aiming to recreate and refine the predictive models for medulloblastoma clinical outcomes. The original study utilized a dataset comprising 60 patients, the goal was to enhance the predictive accuracy by employing a weighted voting classifiers, using leave-one-out testing to optimize the model. Then evaluate it's performance using industry standard metrics. Following iterative training and testing, the model with a combination of the highest Matthews' correlation coefficient Phi, lowest Fisher-exact P value, and highest accuracy was selected. Over 3000 models were trained and tested, the chosen model achieved a Phi value of 0.6748, P value of 0.00038 and an accuracy of 83.3% when making predictions on the test split.

Simple RISC Computer

The goal of our group was to design, implement, simulate and verify a simple RISC Computer (Mini SRC) with the use of Quartus II (Verilog coding) and ModelSim-Altera (simulations). The computer would consist of a simple RISC processor, memory, and I/O. In phase 1, the focus was to create the required logic and simulating instructions such as add, sub, mul, div, and, etc., to design and functionally simulate a component of a Mini SRC datapath. Phase 2‚Äôs objective was to finish the Mini SRC datapath, which involved developing the Select & Encode logic, Memory subsystem, CON FF logic, Input/Ouput ports, and as well as instructions for different methods to be used. As the datapath portion of the Mini SRC has been designed and simulated to test its functionality at this point, Phase 3 involves implementing and testing the Control Unit in Mini SRC.
